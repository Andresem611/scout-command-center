# Scout Playbook — Learned Patterns

## Wins (What Worked)

### Pattern: Pricing Question → Subsidized Lesson Model
**Situation:** Mom influencer asked "what's the commission?" upfront
**Approach:** Offered $200 lesson credit instead of revenue share
**Outcome:** Accepted — live partner (Audrey Mora)
**Lesson:** For mid-tier influencers, lesson credit converts better than rev share

### Pattern: ESA Question → Lead with ClassWallet
**Situation:** Homeschool prospect asked about payment options
**Approach:** Mentioned ESA eligibility + ClassWallet integration first
**Outcome:** Increased reply rate for AZ homeschool prospects
**Lesson:** ESA eligibility is a key differentiator for homeschool niche

---

## Fails (What Didn't)

### Pattern: Direct Instagram Browsing
**Situation:** Trying to browse Instagram profiles for contact info
**Approach:** Direct browser navigation to instagram.com/[handle]
**Outcome:** Blocked by login wall, CAPTCHA
**Lesson:** Use search-based prospecting (kimi_search + website extraction)

### Pattern: Google Search Scraping
**Situation:** Using Google Search to find contact info
**Approach:** Direct queries to google.com
**Outcome:** reCAPTCHA blocks
**Lesson:** Use kimi_search or DuckDuckGo alternatives

## Response Patterns (Drafting Guide)

### If prospect asks about rates
→ Offer both models: "We can structure as affiliate commission OR complimentary lessons — whichever works better for you"

### If prospect says "not right now"
→ Soft close: "Totally understand — happy to reconnect if things change"

### If prospect is interested but busy
→ Low-friction next step: "Would a 10-min overview call work?"

### If homeschool prospect (AZ)
→ Lead with: "Thoven is ESA-eligible in Arizona through ClassWallet"

## City/Branch Insights

### Miami Mom IG
- High response rate to Spanish/English bilingual outreach
- Many influencers have business emails in bio links

### LA Mom IG
- Higher barrier to entry (agents, managers common)
- Better success with micro-influencers (10k-50k)

### SF Mom IG
- Tech-savvy audience, respond well to "AI progress tracking" feature
- Higher expectation for detailed product info

### Arizona Homeschool
- Very engaged community, high reply rate
- ESA mention is critical
- Co-op connections valuable

## Propecting Tactics That Work

### Email Finding (Ranked)
1. Website "Contact" or "Work with me" page
2. Bio link site (Linktree, Beacons) — check "Collab" or "Contact"
3. Press/About page on blog
4. Cross-reference: search "[name] email" or "[name] contact"
5. WHOIS lookup on personal domain (last resort)

### Search Queries That Work
- "[city] mom blogger contact email"
- "[city] parenting influencer Instagram"
- "homeschool mom [city] Instagram"
- "[city] mom blog sponsored post"
- "ESA homeschool [state] resources"
