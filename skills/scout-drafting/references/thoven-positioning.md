# Thoven Positioning Guide

How to talk about Thoven in outreach emails.

## What Thoven IS

**One-line:** Thoven is a music education marketplace connecting families with quality music instruction.

**For parents:**
- Music education without the scheduling nightmares
- Self-paced lessons that fit into 15-minute windows
- Parent-guided but kid-driven
- Affordable alternative to private lessons

**For teachers:**
- Keep your autonomy and set your own rates
- Not a race-to-the-bottom gig platform
- Flexible scheduling that respects your time
- Focus on quality, not quantity

**For partners (influencers):**
- Genuinely helps their audience (not just another product)
- Solves a real pain point (music = hardest subject to teach at home)
- Flexible partnership structures
- Values-aligned (education-first, not entertainment-first)

---

## What Thoven is NOT

| Don't Say | Instead Say |
|:----------|:------------|
| "Music matching service" | "Music education marketplace" |
| "Uber for music lessons" | "Platform for independent teachers" |
| "App for kids" | "Music education platform" |
| "Just another ed-tech startup" | "We're building something different" |
| "Cheap lessons" | "Affordable alternative to private instruction" |
| "Screen time activity" | "Active music-making with minimal screen time" |

**Critical distinction:** We are NOT a matching service that commoditizes teachers. We are NOT a consumer app that entertains kids. We are an education platform.

## Key Messages

### For Parents
1. **Time:** "Fits into your rhythm, not fights against it"
2. **Independence:** "Kids can do it on their own"
3. **Affordability:** "Fraction of private lesson cost"
4. **Quality:** "Designed by educators, not game designers"

### For Teachers
1. **Autonomy:** "You set your rates, you choose your students"
2. **Respect:** "Not a gig platform, a professional marketplace"
3. **Flexibility:** "Teach on your schedule, not someone else's"
4. **Quality:** "We attract families who value education"

### For Partners
1. **Alignment:** "We're selective — we only partner with people who genuinely care"
2. **Value:** "This solves a real problem your audience faces"
3. **Flexibility:** "We're open to what works for your community"
4. **Long-term:** "Looking for relationships, not transactions"

## Partnership Models

### Subsidized Model
We offer discounted or free access to the platform for the influencer's family, plus codes for their audience.

**Pitch:**
> "We'd love to give your family complimentary access to Thoven, plus a discount code for your community. No strings, just want you to experience it before recommending."

### Revenue Share Model
Partners earn a percentage of revenue from customers they refer.

**Pitch:**
> "We're flexible on structure — revenue share, affiliate, or something custom. What works best for how you typically partner?"

### Content Collaboration Model
Co-create content featuring Thoven (not just sponsored posts).

**Pitch:**
> "We're interested in real content collaborations — maybe a 'day in the life' showing how your family integrates music learning. Nothing scripted, just authentic."

## Keri's Voice

**Tone:** Warm, direct, educator-first (not salesperson)

**Characteristics:**
- Uses contractions ("I'm", "we've", "it's")
- Short sentences
- Asks genuine questions
- No corporate speak
- Shows personality ("No pitch, just curious")

**Examples of Keri's voice:**
- ❌ "We would be delighted to discuss a potential synergistic partnership"
- ✅ "Would you be open to a quick chat?"

- ❌ "Our platform leverages cutting-edge pedagogy"
- ✅ "We've solved that with bite-sized lessons kids can do independently"

- ❌ "Please let me know your availability at your earliest convenience"
- ✅ "Available for a brief call this week?"

## Red Flags to Avoid

**Words/phrases that trigger spam filters:**
- "Opportunity"
- "Exclusive offer"
- "Limited time"
- "Act now"
- All caps
- Multiple exclamation marks

**Phrases that sound like MLM:**
- "Join our team"
- "Be your own boss"
- "Unlimited earning potential"
- "Work from anywhere"

**Tone mismatches:**
- Too formal (sounds like a corporation)
- Too casual (sounds like a random DM)
- Too pushy (multiple CTAs)
- Too vague (no specific reference to their content)
