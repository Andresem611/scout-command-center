# Personalization Patterns

How to research prospects and personalize outreach authentically.

## What Details to Grab

### From Bio/Profile
| Detail | Why It Matters | How to Use |
|:-------|:---------------|:-----------|
| **Location** | Local references build connection | "I noticed you're based in Austin..." |
| **Kids' ages** | Determines product relevance | "With kids around that age..." |
| **Years homeschooling** | Shows experience level | "After 5 years of homeschooling, you probably..." |
| **Content themes** | Identifies values alignment | "Your focus on child-led learning..." |
| **Profession** | Context for their perspective | "As a former teacher yourself..." |

### From Recent Content (Last 10-15 posts)
| Detail | Why It Matters | How to Use |
|:-------|:---------------|:-----------|
| **Specific topics** | Shows current interests | Reference in opening line |
| **Engagement style** | Reveals tone preferences | Mirror their communication style |
| **Comment themes** | Identifies audience pain points | "I noticed your followers often ask about..." |
| **Posting frequency** | Shows how active they are | Don't reference 3-month-old content |
| **Format preference** | Video vs. photo vs. carousel | Reference the actual content |

### From Engagement
| Detail | Why It Matters | How to Use |
|:-------|:---------------|:-----------|
| **Top performing posts** | Shows what resonates with their audience | "Your post about X clearly struck a chord..." |
| **Common questions** | Reveals audience needs | "Seems like your community struggles with..." |
| **Values expressed** | Determines alignment | "I love how you prioritize..." |

## How to Reference Content Authentically

### The 3-Rules for Content References

1. **Recent:** Reference content from the last 2-4 weeks (shows you're actively following)
2. **Specific:** Reference actual details, not generic praise ("your morning routine hack" not "great content")
3. **Relevant:** Connect to Thoven's value proposition (don't force it)

### Good Examples

**Specific and recent:**
> "I just saw your post about your homeschool morning routine — the way you described the transition from breakfast to lessons really resonated. That's exactly the kind of rhythm Thoven is designed to support."

**Shows you've done homework:**
> "Your recent series on screen-free activities has been so helpful — I especially loved the post about rainy day ideas. Music is often overlooked in those lists, which is why I thought of you."

**Connects values:**
> "I noticed you've been sharing more about child-led learning lately. That's core to how we built Thoven — kids choose their pace and interests, parents facilitate."

### Bad Examples (Don't Do This)

**Too generic:**
> ❌ "I love your content!"

**Outdated:**
> ❌ "That post from last year about piano lessons was great..."

**Forced connection:**
> ❌ "Your cat photos remind me of music education..."

**Excessive flattery:**
> ❌ "You're literally changing the world with your content..."

## Personalization Angles by Prospect Type

### Mom Influencers
**Look for:**
- Ages of kids (Thoven works best for 4-12)
- Complaints about busy schedules
- Requests for activity ideas
- Screen time concerns
- Budget-conscious posts

**Personalization hooks:**
- "With [age] kids, you know how hard it is to find activities that..."
- "I saw your post about being overwhelmed — we've designed Thoven specifically for..."
- "Your followers keep asking for screen-free ideas — music is..."

### Homeschool Influencers
**Look for:**
- Curriculum struggles
- "What about music?" comments from followers
- Year-end reflection posts
- Planning/scheduling content
- Subject-specific challenges

**Personalization hooks:**
- "Music is consistently the #1 gap in homeschool curriculum..."
- "Your planning posts are so organized — music can fit into those 15-minute transition windows..."
- "I noticed a lot of your followers asking about music curriculum..."

### Music Teachers
**Look for:**
- Studio business content
- Frustrations with platforms
- Student success stories
- Teaching philosophy posts
- Professional development

**Personalization hooks:**
- "Your approach to [specific technique] aligns with how we..."
- "I saw you mention the challenges of finding quality students..."
- "Your post about maintaining autonomy as a teacher really resonated..."

## Research Workflow

### Step 1: Profile Scan (2-3 minutes)
- Read bio completely
- Note location, family details, profession
- Check follower count and engagement rate
- Look for pinned/featured content

### Step 2: Content Review (5-7 minutes)
- Scroll last 15-20 posts
- Note themes and patterns
- Identify top-performing content
- Look for comments/questions from audience

### Step 3: Angle Selection (2-3 minutes)
- Choose 2-3 personalization angles
- Pick the strongest content reference
- Draft opening line
- Verify connection to Thoven is natural

### Step 4: Verify Fit
- Does their audience match Thoven's target?
- Is their content quality high enough?
- Would a partnership feel authentic?
- Are they already promoting competing products?

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

### Don't: Fake Intimacy
**❌** "I feel like I know you from following your journey..."
**✅** "I've been following your homeschool content..."

### Don't: Over-Personalize
**❌** "I saw your post on Tuesday at 3pm where you mentioned..."
**✅** "I noticed in your recent content..."

### Don't: Make Assumptions
**❌** "As a busy mom of three, you probably struggle with..."
**✅** "With kids around that age, you might relate to..."

### Don't: Reference Private Info
**❌** "I saw on your Facebook that..." (if reaching out via email)
**✅** Stick to public content on the platform you're referencing

### Don't: Stalk
**❌** Reference content from 6 months ago
**✅** Reference recent, public content only

## Quick Reference: Fit Indicators

| Strong Fit | Weak Fit |
|:-----------|:---------|
| Kids 4-12 | Kids under 3 or over 16 |
| Homeschool content | Pure entertainment content |
| Education-focused | Product-review heavy |
| Engaged community | Low engagement/fake followers |
| Values alignment visible | No clear values expressed |
| Recent consistent posting | Inactive for months |
| Already mentions education gaps | Only promotes paid partnerships |
